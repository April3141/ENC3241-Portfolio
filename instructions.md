# Instructions

[How to Assemble Cake Pops

Step 1. Remove the crusty edges of your baked cake and discard them. (Figures 1 & 2)

 
Figure 1. Baked strawberry cake with crust removed. (Source: April)


 
Figure 2. Strawberry cake crust. (Source: April)

Step 2. Break the cake into fine crumbles. (Figure 3)

 
 
Figure 3. Strawberry cake crumbled into a fine consistency. (Source: April)

Step 3. Line a baking sheet with wax paper.

Step 4. Scoop ¾ of a tub of frosting into the crumble. (Figure 4)

 
Figure 4. Strawberry frosting tub illustrating what is left after use. (Source: April)

Step 5. Stir the frosting into the crumble using a spoon.

Step 6. Using a spoon, scoop the mixture.

Step 7. Using 2 hands, gently roll the scooped mixture into 1.5” balls.

Step 8. Place them on the wax paper lined baking sheet.

Step 9. Repeat steps 6 through 8 until there is no mixture left. (Figure 5)

 
Figure 5. Cake balls placed on baking sheets lined with wax paper. (Source: April)

Step 10. Place the baking sheets in the freezer for 30 minutes.

Step 11. Melt melting chocolate of choice according to package instructions. (Figure 6)

 
Figure 6. Melted chocolate and preferred brand. (Source: April)

Step 12. Remove cake pops from freezer.

Step 13. Dip treat stick into chocolate.

Step 14. Insert chocolate dipped treat stick into cake pop.

Step 15. Place cake pop on baking sheet.

Step 16. Repeat step 13 through 15 for the remainder of the pops.

Step 17. Place the baking sheet(s) into the refrigerator for 5 minutes.

Step 18. Remove the cake pops from the refrigerator.

Step 19. Dip the cake pops into the melted chocolate using a spoon to help with the top.

Step 20. Place the cake pops on the wax paper lined baking sheets. (Figure 7)

 
Figure 7. Dipped cake pops (I didn’t have treat sticks). (Source: April)

Step 21. Refrigerate the cake pops for 5 minutes to complete the process.
]
