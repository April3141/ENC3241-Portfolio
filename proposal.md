# Proposal

Electroculture Gardening Proposal
April Lamoureux						Integrated General Studies

Background
Electroculture gardening is a controversial topic which is popular among the blogging and DIY garden community; however, it does not have significant amounts of scientific research to substantiate claims that it works. Does the electroculture gardening method increase crop yields and reduce pest problems in the garden? I am proposing an experiment that will gain data to contribute to the validation of such claims. Copper wire is wound in a clockwise direction around a wooden stick and inserted into the ground at the north and south ends of the garden. This acts as an antenna which will harness the electrical currents in the atmosphere.

Methodology 
Two identical raised garden beds will be constructed with 5 feet of distance between them. A cattle panel will be wedged between them, not penetrating the ground, creating a trellis for vining vegetables to grow on while 5-foot poles, inserted 1.5 feet into the ground, and safety wire will be used to secure the trellis in place. Copper wire will be wound around two 6-foot-long garden bamboo sticks and installed to the depth of the garden bed in the north and south end of the garden bed. To ensure one variable is under test, both garden beds will house the same type and number of plants through the growing season.

Anticipated Outcomes 
This project will result in access to a data set for the gardening and scientific community on electroculture gardening and determine whether electroculture gardening is a valid method to a higher crop yield and pest control.

 
Significance 
•	The results of this project will benefit the gardening and scientific communities, by way of introducing a new data set, as well as new gardeners. If electroculture gardening claims can be proven valid it would benefit anyone that would like to grow a vegetable garden using less pesticides.
•	This work will benefit the gardening, scientific, and DIY gardening communities by way of introducing a new data set.
•	By executing an experiment which uses a control, an identical garden bed with no copper wire, the difference in plant growth will either prove or disprove the theory of electroculture gardening.
